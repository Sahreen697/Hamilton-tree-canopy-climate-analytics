## Project Overview

This award-winning project analyzes the impact of climate change on Hamilton's urban tree canopy using City of Hamilton open data. The analysis combines environmental, geographic, and socioeconomic data to evaluate canopy coverage, climate resilience, and environmental equity across city wards.

## Business Problem

Urban trees provide important environmental benefits, including carbon sequestration, stormwater management, heat reduction, and improved air quality. However, tree distribution is often uneven across communities. This project examines whether Hamilton's tree canopy is equitably distributed and identifies areas where additional investment may improve environmental resilience and community well-being.

## Key Insights

* Hamilton's canopy coverage remains below the city's long-term target.
* Lower-income wards generally had fewer trees than higher-income wards.
* Certain species dominate the urban forest, creating biodiversity risks.
* Tree canopy distribution varies significantly across wards.
* Strategic planting can improve climate resilience and environmental equity.
